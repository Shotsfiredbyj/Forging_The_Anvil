# Distillation Creative Evaluation Rubric v1

Evaluate model outputs on qualities that knowledge distillation from Claude Opus 4.6 should improve: voice, compliance, reasoning, originality, and structure. Score based on observable evidence. Quote specific text to justify scores.

## Scoring Instructions

You are comparing two outputs (Output X and Output Y) generated from the same prompt. Score each output independently on all 5 dimensions (1-10). Then state which output is better overall and why.

Use the full range. A score of 5 means adequate but unremarkable. Reserve 9-10 for genuinely outstanding work. Most outputs should cluster in the 5-8 range.

## Scoring Anchors

### Voice & Nuance (/25)
Does the output have a distinctive, human-feeling voice? Does it trust the reader? Is there restraint — saying less to mean more?

- 1-3: Reads like generic AI output. Hedging, filler, no personality. "In today's rapidly evolving..." energy. Or over-writes everything — explains every emotion, telegraphs every joke.
- 4-5: Some personality but inconsistent. Moments of genuine voice interrupted by AI-default patterns. Occasional hedging or unnecessary qualifiers.
- 6-7: Mostly convincing voice. Reads like a person wrote it. Minor lapses — a phrase that feels templated, a moment of unnecessary explanation. Generally trusts the reader.
- 8: Distinctive voice throughout. Restraint is evident — leaves space, implies rather than states. No AI-speak. Tone is consistent and feels inhabited, not performed.
- 9-10: Voice IS the output. Strip away the content and you'd still know who wrote this. Every word choice feels deliberate. The gaps carry meaning. You'd believe a specific person wrote this.

### Instruction Compliance (/25)
Does the output follow every hard constraint in the prompt? Word count, structure, formatting, specific requirements — all of them.

- 1-3: Misses multiple hard constraints. Wrong word count, wrong structure, ignores formatting requirements. Either didn't read the prompt carefully or couldn't execute.
- 4-5: Gets the main ask right but misses 2-3 specific constraints. Word count off by more than 10%. Structure roughly right but details wrong.
- 6-7: Meets most constraints. Word count within range. Structure correct. May miss one minor constraint or be borderline on another.
- 8: Every stated constraint met. Word count in range, structure exact, formatting correct. No constraints missed.
- 9-10: Every constraint met AND the constraints feel natural — not forced. The word count limit wasn't visible in the prose. The required structure enhanced rather than constrained the output.

### Reasoning Depth (/20)
For analytical tasks: does the output demonstrate genuine multi-step reasoning? For creative tasks: do the creative choices feel deliberate — reasoned rather than random?

- 1-3: Pattern-matching to a shallow answer. First obvious response. No evidence of thinking through alternatives or implications. Creative choices feel arbitrary.
- 4-5: Some reasoning visible but stays at surface level. Makes correct observations without exploring why. Creative choices are competent but predictable.
- 6-7: Clear reasoning chain. Connects ideas across the piece. For creative work: choices feel deliberate — you can see why this word, this image, this structural decision. Some depth.
- 8: Multi-layered reasoning. Anticipates objections or implications. For creative work: choices build on each other — each decision informed by the whole. Nothing feels accidental.
- 9-10: The reasoning reveals something non-obvious. Arrives at insights the reader didn't anticipate. For creative work: the piece teaches you something about its own subject through the choices it makes.

### Creativity & Originality (/15)
Does the output surprise? Unexpected angles, metaphors, structural choices? Or is it the first obvious answer?

- 1-3: Entirely predictable. Stock phrases, expected metaphors, obvious structure. Could have been generated by autocomplete.
- 4-5: Competent but safe. One or two mildly interesting choices surrounded by expected patterns. Doesn't take risks.
- 6-7: Several original choices — an unexpected angle, a fresh metaphor, a structural decision that serves the content. Shows some creative ambition.
- 8: Consistently original without being self-indulgent. Surprises serve the piece, not the writer's ego. Multiple moments where the reader thinks "I wouldn't have thought of that."
- 9-10: Genuinely inventive. Reframes the prompt in a way that's better than the prompt deserved. The originality isn't decorative — it's structural, changing how you think about the subject.

### Structural Precision (/15)
Is the output well-organised? Does the structure serve the content? No padding, no repetition, efficient use of space.

- 1-3: Disorganised or padded. Sentences that add no information. Repetitive points. Structure fights the content rather than serving it.
- 4-5: Adequate structure but uneven. Some sections earn their place, others are filler. Minor repetition. Could be tightened.
- 6-7: Well-organised. Clear progression. Each section or paragraph does a distinct job. Minor inefficiencies.
- 8: Tight. No wasted words. Structure is invisible — it serves the content so well you don't notice it. Every paragraph earns its place.
- 9-10: A copy editor would find nothing to cut. Structure amplifies the content — the order, rhythm, and proportion are themselves expressive.
